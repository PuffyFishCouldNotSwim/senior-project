Quick Primer

Tariffs – import taxes – were once a niche policy lever. Between 2018 and 2025 they resurged as a centre‑piece of U.S. economic statecraft. Trump’s first term saw targeted duties on steel, aluminum, and Chinese tech goods; his return in 2025 delivered a sweeping 10 % universal tariff plus surcharges up to 125 % on “worst offenders.”

Big Stat: By April 2025, tariffs touched ≈71 % of all U.S. goods imports – a reach unseen since 1930.

2025 : Tariff Shock 2.0

Using IEEPA emergency powers, President Trump imposed a universal 10 % tariff, 25 % duties on NAFTA partners (with USMCA carve‑outs), and escalated China surcharges to an effective 125 %. Market turmoil forced partial pauses by May.

Glossary of Trade Terms

Section 232

U.S. statute allowing tariffs or quotas on imports that threaten national security.

Section 301

Authorises action against unfair foreign trade practices, incl. IP theft (used heavily vs China).

IEEPA

International Emergency Economic Powers Act – lets a President regulate commerce in a national emergency.

Tariff‑Rate Quota

Dual‑level duty: lower rate within a quota volume; higher once exceeded.
