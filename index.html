Why Tariffs?

Tariffs—taxes on imported goods—serve as leverage in trade disputes. President Donald J. Trump revived them as a signature America First tool, arguing they protect U.S. jobs and sovereignty.

Key metric: By April 2025, ≈ 71 % of U.S. imports face a tariff—the most since 1930.

Non‑USMCA goods from Canada/Mexico.

Economic Impacts

Boston Fed estimates Feb 2025 tariffs add 0.5–0.8 pp to CPI. Yale Budget Lab: full 2025 package +2.3 pp inflation (~US$ 3,800/household). Penn Wharton: long‑run GDP −6 %.

Global Populism

Trump’s tariffs energize economic nationalists yet strain allies. EU weighs retaliation; China accelerates self‑reliance.
